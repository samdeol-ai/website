Theater

A life lived on stage
and behind it

Writing, directing, producing — a chronological record of the productions and moments that shaped how I think about leadership, collaboration, and craft.

Theater has been my longest-running creative pursuit. Each production has taught me something I couldn't have learned in a meeting room — about ambiguity, about timing, about trust.

This page grows as I revisit older work and add new chapters. Newest entries appear on top.

January 2012

Shodh — A Farmer at the Edge

Wrote and directed a drama set against the farmer-suicide crisis. The first half lives in silence — a man, a wife, and the slow weight of a failing field.

January 2012

Shodh — The Flock that Held Him Back

Then the sheep and goats arrive — odd, masked, almost mythic — and pull him back from the edge. The play won Best Director; script took runner-up.

August 2009

When the Forefathers Spoke

Wrote, directed, and acted in a comedy-drama about a couple tempted to sell their inheritance — until their forefathers return to remind them what tradition is actually worth.
